Keep your web presence ONE step ahead with Responsive and Adaptive Web Design




 Caption: Responsive has been the top subject these days. Ethan Marcotte coined this term and the debate has raged on. Mobile versus PC! What is actually the jargon about?

The catalyst behind this school of thought is "mobile" or "mobility". Designers have been thinking about what is the right way to design web site where it can fits all device sizes, to enable easier data consumption on multiple devices and equal access to the best-possible, full Internet experience.

The ingredients for Responsive Web Design...
  • CSS3 Media Queries – use it to adapt to different devices and multiple layout designs.
  • Flexible Grid
  • Flexible/Fluid Images
 Do not confuse responsive design with mobile design. Use what is best for your brand and content. According to Pinchzoom, responsive design is just the using of different innovative techniques and technologies to layout adaptability.

Lindy Roux puts it nicely that it is design that responds appropriately to the resolution device and the environment where you consume contents. The content is responsive to the resolution device and size you are watching it on. The core is content design centric as oppose to device design as it is a more flexible approach to match the new resolutions and sizes of the devices.

Multiple designs which is device specific.

However, there may still be some of you out there who are still asking the simple question, ‘but is it worth it?’ And it's actually a very valid question, especially in those circumstances — such as when you or your company are adapting to a RWD workflow — when it can equate to more time and greater costs.

The answer, as always, is: ‘it depends’

Some desktop-optimised designs work fine just as they are on tablets, which was one of GoCardless' main points, and probably the main reason they saw RWD as simply designing for mobile. But aside from just telling you that responsive sites are far more likely to be future-proof as the plethora of computing devices grows, it’s worth looking at some actual statistics.

1.Electric Pulp recently evaluated the affect that responsive-ising a client’s e-commerce site had on conversions, transactions, and revenue.

What's next?

One thing for sure about Responsive Web Design, is that it's not slowing down. In fact, it's evolving. Soon we'll see a time, just like Content Management Systems, where all sites will be expected to be Responsive.
